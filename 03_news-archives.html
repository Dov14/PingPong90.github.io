New Lawyers Join Firm
Nielsen, Zehe & Antas, P.C. is pleased to announce that attorney's Kevin M. Phillips, Marc F. Katalinic, and Robert J. Lentz have joined the firm as Of Counsel. All three attorneys, who were Partners at their last firm, Fisher Kanaris, bring significant experience and accomplishments in a variety of litigation and corporate fields. Nielsen, Zehe & Antas, P.C. welcomes Kevin, Marc and Rob to the firm.

Mr. Phillips brings extensive experience in the areas of warehouse legal liability defense, cargo/transportation, professional liability, construction and subrogation matters. He earned his Juris Doctor from John Marshall Law School. He is a member of The International Warehouse Logistics Association (IWLA), International Association of Refrigerated Warehouses (IARW) and Warehouse Education Research Council (WERC) and serves as General Counsel for the IWLA.

Mr. Katalinic's brings to the firm years of litigation and subrogation experience. He earned his Juris Doctor from John Marshall Law School and is a member of the National Association of Subrogation Professionals. Mr. Katalinic has lectured for clients and trade organizations on such topics as Loss Scene Investigation, Selection and Retention of Experts, Spoliation of Evidence, Subrogation on Products Liability Theories, Subrogation on Contribution Claims and Subrogation on Workers Compensation Claims.

Mr. Lentz brings to the firm significant experience in subrogation matters including areas of fire causation and fire spread, product design and malfunction, construction losses, transportation and warehouse losses, and worker's compensation subrogation matters. He earned his Juris Doctor from John Marshall Law School and is a member of the National Association of Subrogation Professionals.

Nielsen, Zehe & Antas, P.C. is thrilled to announce that Kevin, Marc, and Rob have joined us as Of Counsel and brought their extensive experience to our firm. Jeff Zehe and Brian Cunningham win trademark trial against national retailer
Jeff and Brian successfully defended a local area businessman against national auto parts retailer and mega-chain AUTOZONE alleging trademark infringement. After a bench trial, Judge Darrah of the Northern District of Illinois ruled in favor of our client, Michael Strick, finding no likelihood of confusion between the parties' marks and that the affirmative defense of laches also precluded AUTOZONE's claims.
